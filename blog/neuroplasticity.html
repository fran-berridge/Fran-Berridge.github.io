Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity

  1. Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.
    Neuroplasticity broken down refers to "Neuro" the nervous system consisting of the brain and spinal cord and plasticity referring to "change". Brain plasticity allows you to learn new skills, gather and use new information and even has the ability to help you recover from brain injury.


  2. Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.
    Every time you learn a new capability or skill your brain creates new connections. Repeating those actions reinforces the connections which enable you to remember what you have learnt. Within this course I aim to code every day. This repetition will not only support my understanding of the new concepts I am learning but also exercise my brain and strengthen my memory when it comes to learning coding languages.

  3. What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?
    Meditation is one way that I can support positive neuroplasticity changes within my brain. Meditation, specifically mindfulness practice has been shown to enhance focus and attention and prevent cognitive decline. By completing a 'Daily Calm" meditation for 10min each day prior to completing my study I can help calm my mind and allow my mind to open to the new learning and concepts for the upcoming sprints.
