Explain a Time

  1. When you were blocked on a simple problem
    I came across a simple problem in the first activity of the Javascript-Kata challenge as I did not realise that we needed to use dot notation. The problem arose as when I started my Javascript journey, I had been completed exercises using both dot notation and then bracket notation without realising I was using two different forms of notation.
    When starting the first activity I used bracket notation as in the previous exercise I had been researching Javascript methods and they had all been written in bracket notation.
    This was an interesting problem because I thought that I was writing my Javascript code incorrectly. Problems solving methods I used were googling, trying something, attempting to read error messages, asking for help from peers and coaches and moving onto the next question while waiting for support.
    I was able to get advise from a tutor who explained the difference between dot and bracket notation. I had also reached out to one of my peers who had also used bracket notation so was able to share my newfound knowledge of dot notation. This meant I could go through all my answers and modify them to bracket notation.
    From this activity I should have learnt the difference between dot and bracket notation. I would also like to have learnt to ask questions earlier but I came across another problem later in the kata and was reluctant to ask for help. I will explain this in more detail under the section ‘Explain a time when you were reluctant to ask for help’.


  2. Elegantly(?) solved a problem
    In this example, I am meant to explain a time when I elegantly solved a problem. Personally, I would use the adjective ‘Elegant’ to describe my methods for solving any of the Javascript-Kata exercises. I will instead discuss a time I was ‘successful’ in solving one of the exercises using problem solving methods.
    During the ‘whereAreYou’ activity I was asked to split a string into individual words before filtering out the buzzing words. I used my prior knowledge of the exercises to copy and paster the index.test.js information to my de.Bee page as a guide to what I was meant to complete and then read a few google pages to understand the method of splitting strings into individual words. With this information and a few syntax corrections from ChatGPT I was able to complete the activity without needing ask for support which felt amazing. This made me feel elevated and like I could succeed in writing some Javascript code.

  4. Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so. What might you try differently next time?
    In sprint 4, I spent time working through and researching the human skills section of the sprint. I really enjoyed completing research and learning about how the brain functions and techniques that can be used to logically work through problems. When I started working on the tech challenge, I looked at the foundations helpdesk and it looked like everyone was further ahead and understanding simple concepts om javascript that I was still learning. This made me feel constantly behind and I never really caught up with the group. Next time I need to work on the tech challenges first and ask for help earlier so I feel less pressure and anxiety when working through technical challenges.
