My Learning Plan

Bootcamp Learning Plan

  1. What is your long term goal and/or career pathway??
    I enrolled in this course to obtain the technical and soft skills I need to become a junior developer. My hope this course will enhance my capability within web development and human skills. This will ensure that I not only meet entry level requirements for the role but also add value and support a positive team culture.


  2. What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?
    After 5 weeks of foundations learning I have re-evaluated my strengths and limitations for Bootcamp. A strength outlined in my original evaluation was that I was inquisitive and enjoyed research, analysing information, questioning and collaborating to work through solution designs. I still believe that I have strengths in this area but I now understand that computational thinking is a skill I need to develop.

    Breaking down complex problems using problem solving methods is an important skill within coding. I found during foundations that my limited knowledge of Javascript was a barrier when working through problems. I was able to use other problem solving methods to work towards solutions but this does that additional time. I feel that my technical knowledge will grow over my career but during Bootcamp I will have to focus on understanding the coding basics and seeking peer and tutor support if I am unable to work through errors in my code independently.


  3. What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?
    I think that my biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be to juggle my family life around a full time course. I currently drop my daughter at school every day and when the course begins my daughter will need to walk to school so that I can drive into Wellington. My daughter has had some separation issues in the past so I am nervous that this change in routine will be a difficult transition for her.


  4. What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?
    A non-technical skill I would like to develop on this course is my listening skills. I come from a neurodiverse family and sometimes in group meetings I can unintentionally interrupt people. I find this really embarrassing as within meetings listening skills are crucial. During foundations I enjoyed the listening and looping activity and I hope to develop the skill of mindful listening during Bootcamp.


  5. What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?
    My expectations from the Bootcamp team reflect my previous expectations of the Foundations Team. Questions are an important part of the learning process. If I am confused during the course and ask for support I hope the tutors can support me firstly to understand the problem that is being outlined and secondly can either guide me towards technical learning material or support me in other ways to work through problems I am struggling to comprehend.


  6. What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp
    During Bootcamp I will be based mainly on campus so I will be more proactive in asking for support from peers and tutors. With flipped learning I feel that it is much easier to spend excessive amounts of time trying to work through problems as you are remote. I am committed to attending Bootcamp, engaging in individual, pair and group activities and using the weekend to reflect on what I have learnt during the week. I will also write daily goals in a journal and try to reflect on these at the end of each day.
