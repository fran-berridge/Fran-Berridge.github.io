My Identity and Values

  1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

    Within one of my workplaces one of my colleagues had some health issues. His work was exceptional, although he was introverted he/she still interacted well with colleagues and stakeholders and his/her attendance was not an issue. The manager was clearly not happy about his/her specific health issues but had not raised them with the staff member. One day he/she went over to the colleagues desk and raised an issue in an open plan working environment that should have been raised in private meeting room. When the employer raised their voice the staff member told them in colourful word to leave. I went over to my colleague to see if they were ok and we went into a meeting room to talk. A few days later the manager gave the staff member a formal process. The staff member showed me the letter and I offered to support them through the HR process. This put me in an awkward position as the manager was in my wider team. This is where I had to use my morals and ethics. I had witnessed the incident and although the staff member had used inappropriate language at the end of the incident to tell her to leave. The conversation should never have occurred in an open workspace environment, the manager should not have raised her voice and with the person being an introvert he had tried his best to avoid conflict through words prior to his phase. When I reflect on my decision I feel it was the logical thing to do. Both staff members had a voice in the HR process and giving an hest account of what happened was the right thing to do. Based on the evidence provided HR dismissed the claims and my colleague was able to continue working.


  2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

    I am a mother, a member of the PTSG at my daughters school and now s student with Dev Academy. At my old work we had a work whanau which influenced my decisions both as a member of my team and a leader within our organisation. I try to meet deadlines set within projects because I understand that deliverables are important to meet implementation deadlines. I also am a realist and have worked in a high pressure projects where issues can mean that meeting deliverables are not always possible. In these cases I try and raise issues and adapt deliverable in a collaborate way so teams and key stakeholders have all the information needed to make decisions. Having an open and supportive culture within teams means that issues are able to be escalated and that solutions can be worked through by the team rather than blame being placed on one person.


  3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

    Within the Clifton strengths finder my top strength was Futuristic this is an interesting strength. A description of this strength is that people who have this trait make sense of where we are going today by forecasting that into the future it falls into the strategic thinking category themes. I can use this strength within the course and future employment opportunities to inspire hope by communicating what I envisage to possible in the future. Through blue sky thinking exercises I can support collaborate sessions where the sky is the limit, and we can think outside the box and not be limited by current state IT budget or staff resources. I also feel that communicate is one of my key strengths as I am not scared to reach out for support to enhance community of practice meeting, practice development, capability and tool development.


  4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

    I tend to think outside of the box and sometimes my ideas can mean that MVP timeframe deliverables may be extended for an idea to come to fruition. Although this is a limit for MVP deliverables if we work through sensible project planning and roadmaps some ideas are possible when you look at the overall enhancement to staff capability and productivity.


  5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

    When I was a Practice Lead one of my deliverables was to implement a Capability Assessment for Business Process Management kaimahi. Capability Assessment tools had already been implemented in the Business Analysis and Service Design areas via an Excel spreadsheet. Rather than implementing the exact same tool and just modifying the questions for my Practice I decided to evaluate the tools that were already in use and run brainstorming and prototyping sessions with the BPM Practice to develop a tool that worked best for our Practice. There was some resistance amongst the other Practice Leads to this idea as instead of a quick roll out it meant actually using the BPM methodology to ensure we were going through the discovery, analysis, options, design and implementation framework. Through working collaboratively with Practice Leads, kaimahi and other key stakeholders I was able to develop options for tools including Excel, Microsoft forms and Qualtrics. The end result was that I rolled out a tool through Qualtrics that everyone was happy with. Reflections from my colleagues were that although they were sceptical at the start of the discovery phase through working through the BPM framework and including all stakeholders in the design instead of just rolling out a tool the project had been a success. I had 100% of Kaimahi complete the tool as it was easy, intuitive and only included key questions relevant to BPM Capability Development.
