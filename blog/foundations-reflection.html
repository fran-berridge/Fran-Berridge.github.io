Foundations Reflections
  1. What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

    1. I overthink problems
    2. I need understand the problem before solutionising
    3. I need to work on my time management skills


  2. What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

    Soft skills such as your values, empathy and self-awareness are non-technical skills that valuable for both learning, problem solving and programming. Our empathy and values feed into our emotional intelligence, these are important when building team and stakeholders relationships within a work environment. In relation to programming our empathy can help us to understand the customers' requirements when confirming their wants and needs.


  3. What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

    I found the information about neuroplasticity fascinating. I have always believed in lifelong learning but it was surprising to find out that we exercise the neurons in our brain to strengthen memory and emotional regulation skills. Relating this to learning programming, when learning a new skill you brain creates new neuron pathways, which make new connections. This means that repeating new programming actions we have learnt reinforces the connection our brain has made and enhances our brain plasticity.


  4. Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

    Human skills are important when it comes to having empathy, communication skills, a focus on continuous learning, attention to detail, focus, persistence, process management and so much more.
    A quote from founder of this course summs up this question in two sentences:
    "To be a good web developer, study code. To be a great one, study people." Joshua Vial, Founder of Dev Academy


  5. Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

    Human skills are an important part of this course and relate not only to web development but all industries. Often technical skills can be learnt on the job but human skills are developed over time and a lot harder to teach. An example of this in the workplace is the ability to understand Critical Customer Requirements (CCR). Soft skills give employees the ability to ask questions and liase with customers and key stakeholders to ensure customer needs and wants are understood. Computational thinking is also an important skill as this capability enables indivudals and teams to break down complex problems using problem solving methods.
